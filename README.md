The number of modes propagating through the fiber depends on V-number. ## ðŸ§ª PROCEDURE
Keep the optical bread board on a flat and stable table surface so that it will not toggle.

Fix the pre-fitted cylindrical head of the He-Ne laser source onto the surface of the bread board and confirm the rigidity of the mount.

Fix the laser to the fiber coupler mount on the bread board with the base plate oriented towards the He-Ne laser exit.

Turn on the He-Ne laser and locate the beam spot on the central portion of the laser-fiber coupling lens assembly by adjusting the vertical and horizontal travel arrangements. Tighten the screws after alignment.

Look for the back reflection of the He-Ne laser spot from the rod lens of the coupler. If the back-reflected spot is away from the exit of the cylindrical laser head, slowly adjust the four screws on the laser mount to bring it back into the exit hole.

Confirm the central alignment of the laser beam at the exit of the laser fiber coupler using a white card sheet. If the spot is off-center, slightly adjust the screws of the laser mount.

Connect the multimode optical patch cord to the laser fiber coupler exit and fix the other end of the fiber in the fiber-holding stand using the grub screws.

Observe the bright laser beam coming out of the fiber and adjust the exit tip height to at least 50 mm above the white sheet.

Observe the bright circular spot with a laser speckle pattern on the screen. Refine the multimode pattern using the screws on the laser-fiber coupler. After observation, replace the multimode patch cord with a single-mode fiber patch cord.

For the single-mode patch cord, observe the blur patterns such as single circular, two-lobe, three-lobe, and four-lobe patterns by slightly adjusting the Allen screws of the laser-fiber coupler.
